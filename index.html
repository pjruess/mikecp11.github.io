Do you know what to do in case of a flood?

Today the Federal Govenrment offers you these tips. Our thoughts are that they fall woefully short.

1) Move immediately to higher ground or stay on high ground. Where is higher ground?
2) Evacuate if directed. To where?
3) Avoid walking or driving through flood waters. Where will flood waters be?
4) Turn Around, Don't Drown! What if you just never got that close?

Enter the World of HIPPO!

This alert system could very well keep you from encountering the depths of a flood. The aim of this organization is to ensure that you, a lovely citizen of this great planet, can avoid unnecessary hardship, pain and death due to inefficent and spatially irelevent warnings. With this system you will receive personalized alerts and explict directions on how to get you and your loved ones out of waters way. When floods strike HIPPO is here to help. All you need to do is load the webpage and allow the marvels of modern technology to take you away.

Understanding HIPPO

HIPPO is an acronym that stands for "High Inudation Proactive Prevention Operation. The idea here is to translate the science that is being preformed at the National Water Center in Tuscaloosa Alabama and place it in the hands of everyday citizens using their mobile devices.

How can you use HIPPO? This seems like it would expensive!

HIPPO is a free service to you sponsered by the National Water Center and grounded in the ESRI online platform. All you need to do to benfit from HIPPO alerts is to follow HIPPO on twitter at @FloodHippo. Our goal is to eventually have this system replace the current warning system used by the National Weather Service. But until then, please use our twitter account.
